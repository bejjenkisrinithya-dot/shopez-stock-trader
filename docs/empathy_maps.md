## Empathy Map Canvas

An empathy map is a simple, easy-to-digest visual that captures knowledge about a user's behaviors and attitudes. It is a useful tool to help teams better understand their users.

Creating an effective solution requires understanding the true problem and the person who is experiencing it. The exercise of creating the map helps participants consider things from the user's perspective along with his or her goals and challenges.

---

## Empathy Map 1: The Trader (User)

### WHO ARE WE EMPATHIZING WITH?
A retail paper-trader who is new to stock trading, wants to learn how market mechanics work, and is looking for a clean, simple, lag-free dashboard to track virtual stocks and virtual balance.

### WHAT DO THEY NEED TO DO?
* View live stock price quotes.
* Search for available stock tickers.
* Place buy and sell orders.
* View their complete transaction history.
* Monitor their total virtual portfolio balance and net worth.

### WHAT DO THEY SEE?
* Intimidating financial web interfaces full of crowded charts, indicators, and jargon.
* Conflicting or unreliable stock reviews online.
* Clunky websites that don't scale well or are hard to navigate on mobile devices.

### WHAT DO THEY HEAR?
* Friends boasting about their high investment returns on modern trading apps.
* Online gurus talking about complex technical analysis patterns that seem impossible to learn.
* Peers complaining about high commission fees and lag in transaction processing.

### WHAT DO THEY SAY & DO?
* Ask: "Is there a simple way to practice paper trading without risking my savings?"
* Search Google for "easy-to-use virtual stock market simulators."
* Keep manual spreadsheet files to log mock trades, often losing track of calculations.

### WHAT DO THEY THINK & FEEL?
* **PAINS**:
  * Fear of losing money or making mistake orders due to confusing layouts.
  * Privacy concerns about setting up credentials on untrusted sites.
* **GAINS**:
  * Desires transparent, immediate trade execution with clear visual cues.
  * Wants a soft-colored, beautiful dashboard showing active gains/losses.

## Empathy Map 2: The Administrator (Admin)

### WHO ARE WE EMPATHIZING WITH?
A platform administrator who runs and moderates the ShopEZ Stock Trading Platform, keeps the catalog clean, adjusts parameters, and monitors system activity.

### WHAT DO THEY NEED TO DO?
* Define the initial list of simulated stock tickers.
* Update simulated stock details (names, starting prices).
* Monitor trades and virtual bank balances to prevent exploits.
* Access logs to trace user activities and ensure system security.

### WHAT DO THEY SEE?
* Server load logs showing high activity when prices update.
* Gaps in listing details if external data sources or local configurations fail.
* Messy, unformatted database collections without a central visualization dashboard.

### WHAT DO THEY HEAR?
* Users complaining about delayed trade updates or missing tickers.
* Database administrators emphasizing the need for audit trails.
* Tech-savvy peers discussing sleek, modern React admin interfaces with dynamic filters.

### WHAT DO THEY SAY & DO?
* Say: "I want to keep our simulated catalog updated and secure without writing code on the fly."
* Manually execute database commands or use a basic interface to manage stock parameters.
* Direct users to support tickets when they report account synchronization issues.

### WHAT DO THEY THINK & FEEL?
* **PAINS**:
  * High friction when updating tickers or moderating users manually.
  * Waste of server resources due to idle/duplicate accounts.
* **GAINS**:
  * A unified admin tab with simple forms to update stock attributes.
  * Clean, real-time visual tables showing recent user trades and system audit logs.
